TRAILS OF DISCOVERY

Greece had always been my dream destination, and our 7-day adventure there was beyond anything I'd imagined. We kicked things off with a quick stopover in London (because who can resist a bit of Big Ben and tea?). After a whirlwind London stay, we hopped on a plane to Santorini, where those iconic blue-domed churches and whitewashed buildings greeted us like we'd arrived at a postcard photoshoot.

Our hotel was so white and elegant, it felt like living inside a giant cloud-no complaints there. Oia's sunsets didn't just take our breath away, they practically slapped it out of us. And don't get me started on the seafood! Grilled octopus and seabass so fresh, I felt like we were dining with Poseidon himself.

Next up, we sailed off on a luxury cruise to Naxos, which was like Santorini's laid-back cousin who skipped the tourist rush. We stumbled upon a hidden village called Halki, where we wandered around like kids in a maze, pretending to be art connoisseurs in local shops. Naxos had this serene, slow pace-perfect for digesting all that seafood.

Then came los, the party island where sleep is apparently optional. After dancing until sunrise, we figured some beach time at Mylopotas Beach was the best way to recover. Between sunbathing and swimming, we debated how the Greeks managed to balance such wild nights with lazy, beautiful days.

Finally, Athens. Ah, the Acropolis, the Parthenon-those ancient wonders made me reflect on how travel doesnâ€™t just expand your horizons; it really stretches your patience when you're scaling ruins in 30-degree heat. But worth it? 100%. (Also we are Mumbaikars, we really cant complain)

We wrapped up the trip flying back to Germany as a stopover, then home to Mumbai, already scheming where to head next. Moral of the story? Keep traveling it's the cure for everything, including your need for a good story to tell. And to end this "Trips are the real life CTRL+ALT+DEL-sometimes you just need to reboot in a new timezone." Ciao until a nextadventure unfolds
